<objective>
Make both runtime consumers (reader and editor preview) execute the new
`mode`/`boundTo`/`fadeIn`/`fadeOut`/`autoFadeAfter` contract identically:
scene-bound tracks auto-stop with a fade when the reader leaves the scene,
continuous tracks keep playing across scene transitions (today's implicit
behavior, now driven by an explicit field), and an optional auto-fade timer
fades a track out after N seconds regardless of scene boundaries.
</objective>

<task type="auto">
  <name>Rewrite useReaderAudio music resolution and scene-bound lifecycle</name>
  <files>hooks/useReaderAudio.ts</files>
  <action>
    Rewrite `resolveRuntimeMusic` (lines 95-121) and `getSceneStartMusicUri`
    (lines 84-93) to use `musicMode`/`musicBoundTo`/`musicFadeIn`/
    `musicFadeOut`/`musicAutoFadeAfter` instead of `musicAction`/
    `musicFadeDuration`. Convert seconds to milliseconds only at the call
    sites into `audioManager.crossFade`/`.stop` (new `fadeInMs`/`fadeOutMs`
    options from 18-00), e.g. `fadeInMs: Math.round(music.fadeIn * 1000)`.

    In `applySceneAudio` (lines 171+): track the *previous* scene's
    `musicBoundTo` in a ref alongside `currentBgmUriRef`. When a new scene's
    resolved music state does not itself specify a track (i.e. no explicit
    music block ran for this scene) and the previously playing track was
    `boundTo === 'scene'`, call `audioManager.stop('bgm', fadeOutMs)` using
    that previous track's `musicFadeOut` and clear `currentBgmUriRef`. When
    the previous track was `boundTo === 'continuous'`, leave it playing
    exactly as today (no new stop call needed — this preserves the existing
    implicit-persistence behavior, now driven by the explicit field instead
    of by accident).

    Add auto-fade-after handling: when a resolved music state has
    `autoFadeAfter` set and the track actually starts (i.e. inside the
    `crossFade`/reuse branch), schedule a single `setTimeout` that calls
    `audioManager.stop('bgm', fadeOutMs)` after `autoFadeAfter` seconds,
    guarded by the same `sceneGenerationRef`/session-validity checks already
    used for the resolved-URI callback so a scene change before the timer
    fires does not stop the wrong track. Clear any previous auto-fade timer
    whenever a new scene's music resolution runs.

    For sound events (`soundEvents` handling around lines 292-330): when an
    event's `mode === 'silence'`, call `audioManager.stop(\`sfx:${event.assetId}\`,
    fadeOutMs)` instead of playing; when `mode === 'track'`, keep existing
    play behavior but pass `fadeIn`/`fadeOut` through in ms.
  </action>
  <acceptance_criteria>
    - A scene with a `boundTo:'scene'` music block, followed by a scene with
      no music block at all, results in the first track fading out and
      stopping (not continuing to play).
    - A scene with a `boundTo:'continuous'` music block, followed by a scene
      with no music block, results in the track continuing to play
      uninterrupted (same as today's behavior).
    - `autoFadeAfter` fades the track out at the specified time even if the
      scene itself is longer, and does nothing if the scene/track changes
      before the timer fires.
    - A `sound` block with `mode:'silence'` stops the named looping sfx
      channel.
  </acceptance_criteria>
</task>
